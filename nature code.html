Difference between Tropical & Temperate Rainforests

TROPICAL RAINFORESTS TEMPERATE RAINFORESTS
Tropical rainforests are found closer to the equator where it is warm. Temperate rainforests are found near the cooler coastal areas.
They experience higher rainfall With 80 or more inches of rain in a single year. The temperate region receives between 30 to 60 inches of rain in a single year.
Tropical rainforests do not have a set of seasons because it is always warm with continuous rain falls. Temperate rainforests have four seasons: spring , summer , fall and winter.
